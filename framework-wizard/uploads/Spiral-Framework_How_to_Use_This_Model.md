How to Use This Model
A Practical Getting Started Guide
Your First Week (Days 1-7)
Day 1: Assemble the Founding Circle
Task: Identify 3-5 people who might be co-founders
Call them individually and ask:
"I'm exploring starting a regenerative community. Are you interested in exploring this together?"
If yes: "Can you make a 4-hour founding meeting next week?"
Outcome: Confirmed attendees for founding meeting
Day 2-3: Prepare the Founding Meeting
Task: Design a 4-hour gathering
Use the template in Section 1.2: Establish Shared Vision (Worksheet 1.2.A)
Prepare:
Comfortable gathering space
Enough food & beverages
Large paper/markers for capturing ideas
Someone to facilitate (can be external facilitator)
Quiet reflection time
Outcome: Meeting logistics ready
Day 4: First Founding Meeting
Task: Run the 4-hour founding circle
Do:
Share individual stories (why you're here)
Dream together (what would success look like?)
Identify shared values
Name differences (don't gloss over them)
Decide on communication method
Commit to next meeting (schedule month of weekly meetings)
Capture:
Written notes on shared vision
List of core values
Names/contact info of committed people
Decision-making approach agreed
Outcome: First shared understanding established
Day 5-7: Follow-up
Task: Document and share what emerged
Create a simple 1-page summary:
FOUNDING CIRCLE SUMMARY
[Date]
Who's on the founding team:
[Names, brief bios]
What we learned about our vision:
[2-3 sentence summary]
Our shared values:
[List of 5-7 values]
How we'll make decisions:
[Your method]
Next meeting:
[Date/time/location]
Email to all attendees asking for feedback.
Outcome: First written documentation created
Your First Month (Weeks 2-4)
Week 2: Individual Vision Interviews
Task: One-on-one interviews with each potential founder
Use: Worksheet 1.1.A: Founder Assessment (from template)
Interview each person (1.5 hours each). Ask:
Why they're drawn to this
Their non-negotiables
What they fear
How much they can commit
What they'd bring
Outcome: Deep understanding of each person, written notes
Week 3: Vision Synthesis
Task: Identify patterns and conflicts
From interviews, create:
List of common themes
List of differences
Major concerns
Non-negotiables (yours and theirs)
Have conversation as full group:
"Here's what I heard..."
"Are these differences dealbreakers?"
"Can we live with these differences?"
Outcome: Clarity on team compatibility
Week 4: Foundational Documents
Task: Draft 3 key documents
Document 1: Founding Values Statement (Worksheet 1.4.A)
One shared vision statement (2-3 sentences)
Top 5-7 shared values
3-5 non-negotiables
Signed by all
Document 2: Operating Agreement (create basic version)
When you meet
How you communicate
How you decide
What to do when someone leaves
1-2 pages is fine
Document 3: Commitment Letter (Worksheet - Founder Commitment)
Each person signs with their commitment
Time available
Financial investment possible
Exit conditions
Outcome: Founding documents in place (even if rough)
Your First 3 Months (Month 2-3)
Task 1: Market Research
Goal: Find evidence that people actually want what you're building
Action:
Identify 10-20 people who match your "ideal community member" profile
Interview them using Worksheet 2.2.A: Demand Assessment
Ask: "If this community existed today, would you be interested?"
Track: How many yes / maybe / no
Success Indicator:
50%+ of people interviewed show clear interest
3-5 people say "I'd definitely consider it"
People can articulate what appeals to them
Outcome: Market validation report (1-2 pages)
Task 2: Research Existing Communities
Goal: Learn from projects similar to yours
Action:
Find 5-10 comparable regenerative communities (IC.org, Cohousing.org)
Research online: how long to populate? What was capital? Financially viable?
Contact 2-3 and have conversations with founders/residents
Document: What worked? What surprised them? What would they do differently?
Questions to Ask:
How long from idea to first occupancy?
How much total capital was needed?
Are you financially sustainable now?
What was harder than expected?
What's your biggest learning?
Outcome: Case study notes (1-2 pages per project)
Task 3: Bioregion Research
Goal: Identify 3-5 potential bioregions for your project
Action:
Use Worksheet 2.3.A: Bioregional Match Analysis
Research climate, water, soil, zoning for each region
Read up on existing regenerative projects in each region
Contact 1 project in each region to learn about living there
Outcome: Bioregion assessment (1-2 pages per region, ranking them 1-5)
Task 4: Financial Reality Check
Goal: Rough estimate of capital needed
Action:
Use Worksheet 2.4.A: Preliminary Financial Model
Make educated guesses on:
Land cost (per acre in target regions)
Infrastructure per person
Housing per unit
Working capital for 2 years
Formula:
Land: ___ acres × $___/acre = $______
Infrastructure: $_______
Housing: ___ units × $_____/unit = $_______
Working capital: $_______
TOTAL: $_______ per resident
Outcome: "Ball park" capital estimate (probably $200K-$5M range depending on scope)
Month 4-6: GATE 1 Assessment
Meeting: Full team gathering
Task: Review progress against Gate 1 criteria
Worksheet: Use Gate 1 Checklist
Questions to ask together:
Is our team alignment real or just surface level?
Have we actually tested our decision-making?
Do we truly believe there's demand?
Are we being realistic about timeline?
Are we still all committed?
Outcomes:
☐ PROCEED to land search (you feel confident)
☐ PAUSE to do more team alignment work
☐ STOP because something fundamental doesn't work
If PROCEED: You're ready for Spiral 2
Spiral 2: Land Search (Months 6-12)
Month 6: Land Search Strategy
Task: Define exactly what you're looking for
Use: Worksheet 3.1.A: Land Search Parameters
Define:
Geographic regions (top 3 choices)
Size (acres needed based on population)
Budget (what can you actually afford)
Essential features (must-haves)
Deal-breakers
Create a one-page "Land Hunting Guide" and share with your network.
Months 6-12: Active Land Search
Task: Look for land constantly
Where to look:
Real estate websites (Zillow, LandWatch, etc.)
Real estate agents (find regenerative-friendly agent)
Word of mouth (tell everyone what you're looking for)
Local networks in target regions
Land brokers specializing in conservation land
For each property you find:
Use Worksheet 3.2.A: Land Identified & Evaluated
Research zoning, water, soil, cost
Visit if it looks promising
Document findings
Timeline:
Months 6-8: Search and research
Months 8-10: Identify top candidates
Months 10-12: Deep due diligence on selected property
Month 9: Financial Planning
Task: Detailed financial model and funding strategy
Use: Worksheet 4.1-4.5 (detailed financial templates)
Create:
Detailed capital needs ($________)
Phased development plan
Operating budget estimate
Revenue model
Funding sources identified
Share with: Financial advisor for feedback
Month 12: GATE 3 Assessment
Question: Is land + team + capital actually converging?
Checklist:
☐ Land identified (you've made offer or signed option)
☐ Team roles filled (architect, engineer, lawyer, CFO identified)
☐ Capital sources identified (not raised, but pathways clear)
Decision:
☐ PROCEED (ready for deep design & capital campaign)
☐ PAUSE (one of the three elements needs more work)
☐ STOP (something fundamental changed)
Spiral 3: Deep Design & Capital Campaign (Months 9-15)
Task 1: Master Plan
Goal: Detailed site design showing all systems integrated
Use: Worksheet 6.1.A: Master Plan Elements Checklist
What needs to be on the master plan:
Building locations (houses, common spaces)
Water system (collection, storage, treatment, distribution)
Energy system (solar, wind, storage, grid connection)
Food system (gardens, orchards, livestock areas)
Waste system (composting, septic, recycling)
Roads and paths
Parking/vehicles
Gathering spaces
Recreation areas
Deliverable: Professional drawing(s) showing integrated design
Task 2: Legal Structure
Goal: Decide how the land will be owned and governed
Use: Worksheet 7.1.A: Organizational Structure Decision
Choose one of:
Community Land Trust (non-profit owns land)
Cooperative (residents collectively own)
LLC (members own)
Hybrid (trust owns land, co-op manages operations)
Hire lawyer to:
Form the entity
Draft bylaws/operating agreement
Ensure tax efficiency
Protect members
Task 3: Capital Campaign
Goal: Raise 70% of capital needed
Use: Worksheets 4.4-4.5: Funding Sources & Investor Pitch
Strategy:
Month 9: Create investor prospectus
Month 10: Identify 10-20 potential investors
Month 11: Pitch to investors (one-on-ones and/or presentations)
Month 12-13: Negotiate terms and close deals
Month 14-15: Deploy capital to contractors/consultants
Task 4: Operational Systems
Goal: Decide how the community will actually run (before it's running)
Use: Worksheet 5.4.A: Operations Manual Outline
Document:
Decision-making process (detailed flowchart)
Meeting structure and frequency
Membership process
Financial management
Communication norms
Conflict resolution (specific steps)
Facility maintenance
Emergency procedures
Month 15: GATE 5 Assessment
Question: Are we funded and operationally ready?
Checklist:
☐ 70%+ of capital secured
☐ Team trained in governance
☐ Master plan complete
☐ Operations manuals drafted
☐ Legal entities established
☐ Permits in process
Decision:
☐ PROCEED (ready to break ground)
☐ PAUSE (one more funding push needed)
Spiral 4: Construction & Activation (Months 12-36+)
This is the longest, most complex phase. Major milestones:
Month 12-18: Phase 1 Construction
Ground breaking
Infrastructure installation (water, energy, roads)
First housing units built
Month 18-24: First Occupancy
First residents move in
Systems tested in real conditions
Governance activated
Culture comes alive
Month 24-30: Phase 1 Completion
All Phase 1 housing occupied
All systems stable
Community culture established
Month 30+: Phase 2+ (if planned)
Additional construction
System refinement
Continuous improvement
How to Actually Use the Templates
When Starting a New Phase:
Step 1: Pick the Right Worksheet
Look at condition set for that spiral
Find the worksheet that matches
Print or open in Google Doc
Step 2: Understand What It's Asking
Read the worksheet through
Understand the "why" (what decision does this help with?)
See examples (they're in the document)
Step 3: Fill It Out As a Team
Don't do this alone
Assign sections to different people
Come back together to synthesize
Make decisions based on findings
Step 4: Document Your Answers
Keep filled worksheets in one place
Update them as facts change
Reference them in future decisions
Use them in presentations to outsiders
Step 5: Use Findings to Make Decisions
"Based on our market research, we believe..."
"Financial model shows we need $X"
"Regulatory assessment shows we can do..."
Red Flags: When to Pause
On Team Alignment:
People not showing up to meetings
Decisions not being honored between meetings
Silent resentment (not expressing disagreement)
One person dominating decisions
→ Pause and do team training/facilitation work
On Feasibility:
Environmental assessment shows problems
Regulatory zoning looks impossible
Market research shows no real demand
→ Pause and reassess (pivot or stop)
On Financial:
Capital raising goes slower than expected
Major investor backs out
Land prices in region spike
→ Pause and revise budget/timeline
On Land:
Owner changes mind about selling
Zoning variance denied
Environmental contamination discovered
→ Pause and search for different land
Key: Pausing is not failure. Pushing forward despite warning signs usually leads to bigger problems.

The Spirit of This Framework
This is not meant to be:
Prescriptive "You must do X"
Rigid "Follow this exact sequence"
Burdensome "All these worksheets are overwhelming"
This is meant to be:
Clarifying "What do we actually need to know?"
Flexible "Adapt to your context"
Empowering "You have the wisdom; these are tools to capture it"
