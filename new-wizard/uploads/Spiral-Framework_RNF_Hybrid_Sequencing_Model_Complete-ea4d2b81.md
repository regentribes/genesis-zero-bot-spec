PART 1: OVERVIEW & PHILOSOPHY
The Hybrid Model at a Glance
This model combines three approaches:
Conditions-Based Foundation - Flexibility in timing; clear prerequisites
Integration Gates - Major decision points; go/no-go clarity
Coherence Spirals - Iterative deepening; build → learn → improve
Why This Works:
Realistic: Acknowledges some activities take predictable time (land search) while others can accelerate
Flexible: Multiple work streams happen in parallel where possible
Safe: Major decisions only made when prerequisites are met
Learning-Oriented: Each spiral provides opportunity to adapt before next phase
Regenerative: Embeds feedback loops and continuous improvement
Core Principle:
"Do reversible things first. Only make irreversible decisions when you absolutely must, and only after thorough validation."

1.2 ESTABLISH SHARED VISION
Worksheet 1.2.A: Individual Vision Interviews
Interview each founder separately, then together. Ask:
1. WHY are you drawn to regenerative community?
   (Personal story, not ideology)
2. What problem are you trying to solve?
   (For yourself / for community / for planet)
3. If this project succeeds beyond your wildest dreams, 
   what would that look like 10 years from now?
   (Be specific - senses, daily life, impact)
4. What are your non-negotiables?
   (Things that MUST be true for you to stay)
   - Things that cannot be compromised on
   - Things that are dealbreakers if violated
5. What are your fears/concerns about this project?
   (Money running out? Wrong people? Failure?)
6. How much risk are you willing to take?
   (On a scale: conservative, moderate, high-risk)
7. What's your timeline expectation?
   (2 years? 5 years? 10 years?)
Synthesis Activity:
Hold a 4-hour founding circle where you:
Share responses (vulnerably, authentically)
Identify common themes
Name differences (don't hide them)
Negotiate on timeline & risk tolerance
Identify what you're all actually trying to create

When to Pause or Stop
PAUSE (take more time) when:
Team alignment is weak - worth investing time here
Market validation unclear - don't guess
Land not right - better to wait than be stuck with wrong land
Capital strategy uncertain - don't start construction underfunded
Governance not practiced - test it first
STOP when:
Fundamental misalignment in team on core values
Market shows no actual demand (interviews say no)
Regulatory environment fundamentally blocks vision
Capital cannot be raised
Land is not available at any price
Continuous Improvement Framework
After each gate:
GATE REVIEW MEETING:
1. ASSESS:
   - Did we meet exit criteria? (yes/no/partial)
   - What evidence supports this?
2. CELEBRATE:
   - What did we accomplish?
   - Who should be recognized?
3. LEARN:
   - What surprised us?
   - What was harder than expected?
   - What was easier than expected?
   - What should we do differently next time?
4. DECIDE:
   - Do we proceed? (yes/conditional/no)
   - If conditional, what conditions must be met?
   - What's the new timeline?
5. COMMIT:
   - Who's responsible for next steps?
   - What's the next checkpoint?
   - How do we stay accountable?
