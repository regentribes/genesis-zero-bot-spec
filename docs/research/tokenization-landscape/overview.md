# Asset Tokenization - Complete Landscape Overview
**Compiled:** 2026-05-02 | **Sources:** Hedera, RWA.io, Davies Group, ACM 2022

## Key Findings

### What is Asset Tokenization
Converting physical/digital assets into digital tokens on blockchain, enabling fractional ownership and frictionless trading.

### Market Size
- Real estate tokenization: $2.7B (2022) → $16T (2030 projected)
- Broader asset tokenization: $3.32B (2024) → $12.83B (2032)
- Source: ACM 2022 proceedings, market research

### Five Token Types (by utility)
1. **Fungible** - interchangeable, divisible (stablecoins, security tokens)
2. **Non-fungible (NFT)** - unique, non-divisible
3. **F-NFT** - fractional ownership of NFTs (art, commercial real estate)
4. **Security tokens** - investment contracts, securities law
5. **Payment tokens** - medium of exchange (BTC, ETH)

### Process (5 Steps)
1. Asset Evaluation - appraisals, audits
2. Legal Structure - SPV/LLC owns asset, tokens = shares
3. Token Creation - blockchain platform (Polygon, Hedera, Ethereum)
4. Smart Contract Implementation - profit distribution, voting, redemption
5. Offering and Management - investor relations, compliance

### Benefits
**For owners:** Increased liquidity, fair prices, reduced costs, composability
**For investors:** Access to high-value assets, shorter lock-ups, transparent history, secure identity

### AML/Regulatory Challenges
- Regulatory uncertainty (SEC, EU MiCA)
- KYC/AML complexity on privacy blockchains
- Complex fractional ownership structures
- Inadequate AML technology for tokens
- Token mixer/tumbler obfuscation

### Integration into Coordination Landscape
- Tokenization enables BlackRock's 50/30/20 private markets thesis
- Palantir ontology can integrate tokenized assets
- Idoneus = luxury asset tokenization with structural red flags
- Integral's OAD = knowledge tokenization without blockchain
